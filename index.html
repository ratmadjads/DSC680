Political News Bias Analysis

The news is a powerful weapon in the age of modern information warfare. State and non-state actors use it to employ time-tested propaganda techniques to yield far-reaching results. Online news is the perfect platform to spread these propaganda messages.

For instance, during the invasion of Crimea, the Russian bots and cyber activists spread propaganda messages through social media and online news to gain public support.

Additionally, the Russian government expands its propaganda messages using news platforms such as R.T. (Russia Today) or WikiLeaks to broadcast fake news or create a wedge between political parties.

Apart from being potential propaganda tools, online news can represent an adequate opportunity to preserve the national security and the strategic interests of a country if appropriately used by civil institutions, in particular, by information security services.

Many government and civilian organizations used online news for content creation, external collaboration, community building, and other purposes.

This milestone project aims to collect news articles related to the middle east regions, such as Afghanistan, Iran, Iraq, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Oman, Palestine, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Syria, Turkey, United Arab Emirates, and Yemen.

Furthermore, to study the events in a particular middle eastern region, study how propaganda penetrates mainstream media outlets, and classify the news article contents.

World News Classification.

This milestone-two project is a continuation of the milestone one project, wherein milestone one, I performed exploratory data analysis to understand the context of my dataset. For milestone two, I build deep-learning and neural network models to process the news contents and understand the sequence of words or characters. There are three fundamental deep-learning algorithms that I use neural networks with dense layers, LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory), and one-dimensional convolutional network. Additionally, I build shallow-learning models, such as the Multi-layer perceptron model and random forest, using the Scikit-learn library. My deep-learning application will classify the news articles into the following categories: weather, finance, current trends, politics, and sports.

Terrorist Attack Classifications and Casualties Predictions in Iraq

The aims of this milestone three are to study the external and internal threats to the Iraqi government and classify whether an attack carried on by a specific terrorist network will be successful or not, using the GTD (Global Terrorism Database) data set provided by the University of Maryland. The GTD dataset consists of publicly available, unclassified source materials collected between 1970-2018, consisting of media articles and electronic news archives, books, journals, and legal documents. Additionally, I will build machine learning models that will predict the number of casualties if the attack was successful based on the attack methods, type of weapons used, and other attributes provided by the GTD data set. To understand the context of this data set, I will perform exploratory data analysis by looking at the correlations between the variables provided in the GTD data set. For example, terrorist networks in Iraq, attack methods (i.e., bombing/explosion, armed assault, assassination, hostage-taking, infrastructure attack, and hijacking), Iraqi cities, Iraqi infrastructures (e.g., government, police, business, school, etc.), and some other factors.
